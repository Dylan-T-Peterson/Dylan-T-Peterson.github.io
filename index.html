FAQ

Q: Why are all of your SQL queries written with SQLite3 syntax?
A: I currently don't have the necessary infrastructure to obtain or maintain an actual server database so instead of leaving out SQL as a whole I decided to use the resources I currently have to practice and maintain my SQL literacy.

Q: What is your development setup?
A: My main workstation is an Ubuntu based setup running I3WM for my window manager and a fork of Neovim that I've heavily customized mainly for Python, SQL, and front-end web development. I tend to modify my configuration files as needed to improve productivity and ease of use when I come across things I could optimize during my projects.

Q: What professional experience do you have in data analytics?
A: I currently have a year of professional experience with about another year of freelance experience. I keep my skills growing with my projects and continue to expand my understanding of analytical techniques/more efficient methods by keeping myself informed and up to date with new program/language releases.

Q: What experience do you have with analytic programs?
A: I have experience in Excel, Tableau, and creating custom scripts using Python. My knowledge of Excel includes use of HLOOKUP/VLOOKUP, INDEX/MATCH, and pivot charts/tables. In Tableau I am able to create cohesive and insightful dashboards and stories. My largest area of expertise comes to the manipulation and analysis of data through customized python scripts and personalized SQL queries, including the manipulation of dataframes through the Pandas module, or database modification and querying through the SQLite3 module. I also have proficiency in the creation of data visualizations using Python's Matplotlib and Seaborn modules.

Q: What experience do you have with different software development lifecycle frameworks and processes?
A: Due to my lack of team based software development I do not currently have hands-on experience with any SDLC frameworks. However, I have kept myself informed about a variety of SDLC frameworks including the Scrum Agile framework, Waterfall, Lean, Iterative, and Spiral.
